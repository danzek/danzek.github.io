Do you want to change children's lives in Chicago?

Posted by Dan O'Day on April 2, 2016

Help me stop literacy roadblocks by joining me in the 2016 Chicago #KFFL campaign. Find out more: http://bit.ly/1UuIHEf #50KBooks

Did you know?

  • The ability to read proficiently by third grade is a key indicator of future success.
  • By fourth grade, only 27% of Chicago’s students are considered reading proficient.
  • A significant factor that contributes to third-grade reading proficiency is access to books.

KPMG founded KPMG Family for Literacy (KFFL) with the mission to improve childhood literacy by providing quality books to children in the communities in which we live and work. This is where you come in—my team's goal is to raise $1,000 in six weeks, which equates to 200 books in the hands of Chicago's youngest readers. I think we can exceed this goal! To get started, I’ve made a donation on the FirstBook website: http://bit.ly/1UuIHEf

I invite and challenge you to donate to this worthy cause. Donations are tax-deductible: http://bit.ly/1UuIHEf
